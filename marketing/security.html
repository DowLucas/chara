Security

Last reviewed: 21 May 2026

We take security seriously because, frankly, our threat model is one bored attacker away from being someone else's bad day. Here's what we do, what we don't, and how to talk to us if you find a hole.

What we do

  • Transport: TLS 1.3 everywhere, HSTS preloaded.
  • At rest: full-disk encryption on Postgres and object storage volumes.
  • Auth: passwordless by default — magic links signed with rotating keys, short TTL. OIDC for self-host; PKCE OAuth on hosted.
  • Sessions: HTTP-only, Secure, SameSite cookies, signed with HMAC-SHA-256.
  • Money math: int64 minor units, decimal strings on the wire. Floats are forbidden.
  • Receipts: object-storage ACLs require a signed URL scoped to the requesting user. URLs expire in 15 minutes.
  • Dependencies: govulncheck in CI, weekly Dependabot reviews, no transitive surprises in the Go module graph.
  • Backups: daily encrypted snapshots, 30-day retention, restore tested quarterly.
  • Access: production access is SSO-gated and audit-logged. Two-person-rule for destructive operations.

What we don't do

  • We don't process payments. We won't ever ask for card data.
  • We don't load third-party scripts on app pages. No tracking pixels, no chat widgets.
  • We don't keep raw email content. Outbound mail is transactional, no marketing pipeline.

Self-host security notes

If you self-host, your security is your call. Some things worth doing:

  • Put the app behind a reverse proxy with TLS (Caddy is the obvious pick).
  • Use a real OIDC provider — Authentik, Keycloak, Authelia, Pocket ID. Don't roll your own auth.
  • Restrict the Postgres port to localhost or your private network.
  • Rotate the SESSION_SECRET regularly; back up ./data regularly.

Responsible disclosure

If you find a vulnerability, please email security@quits.app. We respond within 72 hours and aim to fix critical issues within 7 days. We don't run a paid bug bounty yet, but we credit reporters in the changelog (with permission) and send a small thank-you for valid reports. Please don't open public GitHub issues for security findings.
