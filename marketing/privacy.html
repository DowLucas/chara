Privacy policy

Last updated: 21 May 2026

This policy covers the Quits hosted service at quits.app. If you self-host Quits on your own server, you are the data controller and this policy does not apply — you set your own.

1. Who we are

The hosted service is operated by Quits AB, registered in Stockholm, Sweden. You can reach us at privacy@quits.app.

2. What we collect

Only what's needed to run the product:

  • Account: your email address and display name.
  • Expenses: the amounts, descriptions, dates, currencies, and group memberships you create.
  • Receipts: any photos or PDFs you choose to attach to an expense.
  • Device tokens: an opaque identifier so we can send push notifications, if you enable them.
  • Operational logs: IP, user agent, and request paths, retained 30 days for abuse prevention and debugging.

We do not collect: contacts, location, advertising IDs, third-party analytics events, payment card details (we don't process payments), or anything else not listed above.

3. Why we collect it (lawful basis)

  • Contract (GDPR Art. 6(1)(b)) — to provide the service you signed up for.
  • Legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f)) — operational logs for security and reliability.
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)) — push notifications, optional email digests.

4. Who sees your data

The people you share groups with see the expenses in those groups. That's the product. Nobody else sees your data outside the following processors, all bound by Data Processing Agreements:

  • Hetzner (Falkenstein, DE) — hosting and storage.
  • Postmark (US) — transactional email (magic links, receipts).
  • Expo / EAS (US) — push notification delivery.
  • Cloudflare (US/global) — DNS, edge proxy, DDoS protection. Only request metadata.

We do not sell, rent, license, or trade personal data. We do not run advertising on the service.

5. Where your data lives

Primary storage and backups are in the European Union (Germany). Some processors above are based in the US; transfers rely on Standard Contractual Clauses and supplementary measures as required under EU law.

6. How long we keep it

  • Active accounts: as long as the account exists.
  • Deleted accounts: erased within 30 days, with backups rotated out within 90 days.
  • Operational logs: 30 days.
  • Billing records (where applicable): 7 years, per Swedish accounting law.

7. Your rights

Under GDPR you can: access your data, correct it, export it (CSV or JSON, in-app), restrict processing, object, and delete your account at any time. To exercise these rights, use the in-app controls or email privacy@quits.app. We respond within 30 days. You also have the right to complain to your local supervisory authority — in Sweden, that's IMY.

8. Security

Transport is TLS 1.3. At-rest disks are encrypted. Receipts are stored with object-level access control. Access to production data is limited to the engineers on call and logged. We publish a security overview with our threat model and disclosure process.

9. Children

Quits is not directed at children under 13. If you believe a child has signed up, contact us and we'll remove the account.

10. Changes

If we change this policy materially, we'll email active users and post a notice in-app. The "last updated" date above always reflects the current version.
