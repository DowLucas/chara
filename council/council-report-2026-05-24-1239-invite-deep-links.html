Advisor Responses

The First Principles Thinker strongest · 3 votes

The premise is wrong. You're solving #3 of a 3-step funnel whose #1 (app in store) doesn't exist. Phase 1 ships a landing page whose primary CTAs are dead links. Phase 1 + Phase 2 aren't phases of one project — Phase 1 is "fix the URL-stripping bug and unblock internal testing," Phase 2 is "launch in stores." The "95% of UX" claim is 95% of rendering, 0% of acquisition.

Re-frame: the pre-launch invite primitive that matters is the QR code (phone-to-phone, already works via classifyInvite). Ship a 20-line "paste invite link" field in add-server (currently cut as non-goal — reconsider). Defer the whole landing page until TestFlight submission week.

The Outsider strongest · 1 vote
I tap the link. "You're invited to 'Roommates' · 4 members · hosted at charaapp.lurkhuset.com". I have no idea what I just got invited to.

Roommates what? A chat? A calendar? A Google Doc? Page never says "Chara is a way to split bills with friends." "hosted at lurkhuset.com" reads as phishing. "Open in Chara →" — why is this button here if I don't have Chara?

The four-state copy is written for someone who already knows the product. Add a one-line product tagline above the fold. Drop "hosted at" for normals. Put "Chara" + tagline on the install CTA.

The Executor strongest · 1 vote

Step 1 is the trap. "HTML template asset" hand-waves embed.FS vs filesystem, Docker COPY semantics, badge/asset shipping. Use embed.FS from day one or you'll redo it when Docker breaks.

Step ordering wrong. Do Step 4 (URL shape change in GetInviteLink) first as a 5-min change — unblocks frontend (Steps 6-8) to run in parallel.

Cut from Phase 1: the locked vs archived/invalid split. One "doesn't work" page + ok is enough.

Verification gap: only iMessage→Safari tested. Add Slack/Gmail in-app browser case.

First commit: ten-line diff — change GetInviteLink to HTTPS URL, update test, add GET /i/{token} stub returning 501.

The Contrarian biggest blind spot caller

Phase 2 is a lie. Without Universal Links, every existing user inviting a friend gets shoved through Safari + scheme prompt — the worst path for your most valuable users. Bet you a beer Phase 2 slips six months.

The chara:// button is broken, not "acceptable." WhatsApp/Instagram/Slack/Messenger silently swallow custom schemes in their in-app webview. Spec waves this away with no mitigation.

Decision #6 is a privacy footgun. Bots scrape pastebin/Slack/Discord for /i/<token> URLs. Preview leaks group name + member count publicly. Add X-Robots-Tag: noindex, nofollow and rate-limit. Neither specified.

Self-hosted has no Phase 2 story. Decision #2 says self-hosted gets no Universal Links ever — so self-hosters live in chara:// hell permanently. Self-hosters are who you can't disappoint; that's the whole thesis.

The cold-launch race is shipping as a TODO. "Verify; if it flashes, fix it" isn't a mitigation. Android cold-launch is slower than iOS; the flash will happen.

The Expansionist flagged for blind spot · 3 reviewers

The invite link is the entire top of the acquisition funnel and the only protocol-level handshake Chara has with the outside world. Treating /i/<token> as a tappable fallback is wildly undervalued.

Promote to Phase 1:

  • Open Graph + Twitter cards — iMessage/Slack unfurls into rich preview before tap. 6 meta tags + one OG image endpoint. The difference between "weird link" and "real app."
  • One-line product tagline. The landing page IS Chara's marketing site at launch.
  • Inviter attribution. "Lucas invited you" converts dramatically better. One column, zero rows to backfill pre-launch.
  • Document /i/<token> as a protocol surface — how Matrix and ActivityPub got an ecosystem.
  • Web-join is one step away. Preview endpoint + landing page already exist.

The "non-goal" list is a list of growth levers being declined at the moment they're cheapest to build.

Peer-review consensus: growth thinking for a product that can't yet be installed; OG cards directly contradict the privacy-hardening the Contrarian called for.

Peer Review Highlights

What the council collectively missed

Token enumeration is the actual fatal flaw. The preview endpoint has no auth and no rate limit on a token-shaped URL. Token entropy isn't specified. Bots can scrape and enumerate group metadata across every self-hosted Chara instance via the standardized /i/<token>/preview grammar — and standardizing the protocol (E's proposal) makes it worse, not better.

App Store + Play Console paperwork is the single highest-priority action. 1-3 days. Without bundle ID reservation, AASA/assetlinks can't be valid. The entire spec is theory until these clear.

"Strangers don't tap links from strangers." Real user path: screenshot of the link + "join my Chara" in plain text, then the recipient Googles "Chara app." Spec assumes link-tap as the canonical interaction; scam-trained users do not behave this way.

Inviter-side viral loop missing. Splitwise grew via group creators sharing proactively. Nothing in the spec prompts the creator to share after group creation.

Jargon overload. "Custom-scheme fallback," "Universal Links," "protocol surface," "OG unfurl" — the recipient is someone's mom splitting a dinner bill. The council was designing for developers forwarding links to developers.
